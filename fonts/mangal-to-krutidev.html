What is Mangal Font?

Mangal is a Unicode-compliant Devanagari font that comes pre-installed with Microsoft Windows. It supports the Hindi language and is widely used for displaying Devanagari script on digital platforms. Mangal font follows the Unicode standard, making it compatible across different systems and applications.

What is Krutidev Font?

Krutidev is a popular Hindi font used extensively in government offices, publishing, and printing industries across India. It was one of the first widely adopted Hindi fonts for digital use and remains popular today for its clarity and compatibility with many legacy systems. Unlike Mangal, Krutidev uses a proprietary encoding system rather than Unicode.

Did You Know?

Mangal font is Unicode-compliant and comes pre-installed on Windows systems, while Krutidev uses a proprietary encoding system. Converting from Mangal to Krutidev is often necessary for compatibility with legacy systems and government documents in India.

Why Convert to Krutidev?

  • Legacy System Compatibility: Many older systems still use Krutidev
  • Government Requirements: Official documents often require Krutidev format
  • Printing Industry Standard: Widely used in publishing and printing
  • Familiar Layout: Many Hindi typists are trained on Krutidev keyboard
  • Special Characters: Krutidev has unique character combinations not available in Mangal
