Mangal is the font most people in India use without even realising it, because it comes built into Windows and Microsoft Office by default. Whenever you type Hindi in Word using the standard settings, there is a very good chance it is being typed in Mangal.

But not every system accepts Mangal text. Many older office programs, some government forms, and a large part of India's printing industry still expect Krutidev instead. This converter bridges that gap. Paste your Mangal text, and the Krutidev version appears right away.

Why Mangal and Krutidev Do Not Match

Even though both fonts can display Hindi, they are built on completely different systems underneath. Mangal follows Unicode, the international standard where every Hindi letter has its own fixed code that every device understands the same way. This makes Mangal text reliable and portable.

Krutidev does something different. It uses ordinary English keyboard keys and reshapes them into Hindi letters, but only while the Krutidev font is selected. Paste Mangal text into a Krutidev-based document, and it will not display correctly, because the two systems were never designed to understand each other directly.

This converter solves the problem by properly remapping every character, matra, and joined letter from the Mangal system to the correct Krutidev keystrokes.

Why Mangal Is So Common

Mangal has been the default Unicode Hindi font on Windows for a long time, which means most Hindi text typed casually on a computer ends up in this format, whether the person chose it deliberately or not. It also works well across other apps and devices because it follows the Unicode standard properly.

This is exactly why this converter is framed around Mangal specifically, even though technically it works with any properly typed Unicode Hindi text. Mangal is simply what most people already have open when they arrive here.

A Closer Look at the Two Fonts

Mangal is built for the modern digital world. It works the same everywhere, whether you are on a phone, a laptop, or a tablet, and it does not depend on any special software being installed. It is also friendly to search engines, spell checkers, and accessibility tools.

Krutidev, by comparison, is a font from an earlier era of Hindi computing. It became extremely popular because it made Hindi typing possible on ordinary keyboards long before proper Hindi input methods existed. That popularity means it never fully disappeared, particularly in government offices, exam boards, and the printing industry. Neither font is right or wrong. They simply serve different purposes depending on where your text needs to end up.

Tips for a Smooth Conversion

3. For very long documents, consider converting a smaller test section first to confirm everything looks right before doing the whole file.
