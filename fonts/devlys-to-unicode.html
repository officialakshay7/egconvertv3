What is Devlys Font?

Devlys is one of the most widely used Hindi fonts, especially popular in government offices, newspapers, and publishing houses across India. It was developed as part of the DevLys font family and follows a non-Unicode encoding that maps Devanagari characters to standard ASCII codes. Devlys 010 is the most commonly used variant. Because it uses a proprietary encoding, text in Devlys cannot be displayed correctly without the Devlys font installed — making conversion to Unicode essential for digital use.

What is Unicode?

Unicode is the universal character encoding standard that supports all writing systems. It allows Hindi text to be displayed correctly across all devices, platforms, and applications without requiring special fonts. Unicode is the international standard for text representation and is essential for modern digital communication.
