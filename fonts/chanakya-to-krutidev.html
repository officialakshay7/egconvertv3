What is Chanakya Font?

Chanakya is a popular Hindi font used primarily for printing and publishing purposes. It's known for its distinctive style and is commonly used in newspapers, books, and official documents. Like Krutidev, Chanakya uses a proprietary encoding system rather than Unicode.

What is Krutidev Font?

Krutidev is a widely used Hindi font in government offices, publishing, and printing industries across India. It was one of the first widely adopted Hindi fonts for digital use and remains popular today for its clarity and compatibility with many legacy systems. Krutidev uses a proprietary encoding system rather than Unicode.

Did You Know?

Both Chanakya and Krutidev fonts use proprietary encoding systems rather than Unicode. Converting between them is often necessary for specific publishing requirements, government documentation, or when working with legacy systems that require a particular font format.

Why Convert to Krutidev?

  • Government Requirements: Many government offices standardize on Krutidev
  • Legacy System Compatibility: Works with older administrative systems
  • Wider Adoption: Krutidev is more widely used than Chanakya
  • Better Support: More tools and resources available for Krutidev
  • Familiar Layout: Many Hindi typists are trained on Krutidev keyboard
