What is Unicode to Krutidev Conversion?

If you've ever typed Hindi in a modern app or phone, you were probably using Unicode — the global standard for digital text that works across all operating systems and browsers. It's what powers Mangal, Nirmala UI, and most modern Hindi fonts.

Krutidev 010, on the other hand, is an older, pre-Unicode font system that uses ASCII characters to render Hindi. While it may seem outdated, Krutidev is still the official requirement in many Indian government offices, lower courts, state departments, and traditional print shops — especially across UP, Bihar, Rajasthan, and MP.

The problem? Text typed in Unicode looks like garbled symbols when pasted into a Krutidev document. That's where this converter helps — it accurately remaps every character, matra, halant, and conjunct so your Hindi text looks exactly right in Krutidev format.
